Healthy Food Items

Healthy Foods
1. Fruits (e.g., apples, bananas, berries)
2. Vegetables (e.g., spinach, broccoli, carrots)
3. Whole grains (e.g., oats, quinoa, brown rice)
4. Lean proteins (e.g., chicken breast, fish, tofu)
5. Nuts and seeds (e.g., almonds, chia seeds, walnuts)
6. Legumes (e.g., lentils, chickpeas, black beans)
7. Greek yogurt
8. Olive oil
9. Avocado
10. Salmon

Non-Healthy Food Items

Non-Healthy Foods
1. Fast food (e.g., burgers, fries)
2. Sugary drinks (e.g., soda, energy drinks)
3. Processed snacks (e.g., chips, cookies)
4. Candy and sweets
5. Deep-fried foods (e.g., fried chicken, onion rings)
6. White bread and pastries
7. Ice cream
8. Pizza
9. Alcohol (in excess)
10. Sugary cereals

Community Service Project

Bethamcherla is a gram panchayat located in Bethamcherla Mandal of Nandyal District in Andhra Pradesh. The total population of this village is 2900 and a Government Primary and Secondary School is located. Bethamcherla is a town with Nagar panchayat civic status and mandal headquarters located in Nandyal district of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It comes under Dhone assembly constituency and Nandyal Parliament Constituency.Bethamcherla is a Census Town city in district of Kurnool, Andhra Pradesh. The Bethamcherla Census Town has population of 38,994 of which 19,424 are males while 19,570 are females as per report released by Census India 2011.Population of Children with age of 0-6 is 4882 which is 12.52 % of total population of Bethamcherla (CT). In Bethamcherla Census Town, Female Sex Ratio is of 1008 against state average of 993. Moreover Child Sex Ratio in Bethamcherla is around 940 compared to Andhra Pradesh state average of 939. Literacy rate of Bethamcherla city is 65.60 % lower than state average of 67.02 %. In Bethamcherla, Male literacy is around 75.03 % while female literacy rate is 56.34%.

Survey Details

Healthy eating contributes to an overall sense of well-being, and is a cornerstone in the prevention of a number of conditions, including heart diseases, high blood pressure etc. We conducted the survey in Bethamcherla village, Bethamcherla Mandal, Nandyal District. The main purpose of this study is to determine how the people eating their food, whether they take healthy food or not, in a proper way and to investigate the food choices of different people and the determinates of their food choices in the village of Bethamcherla which is located in Nandyal District. We also determine the food habits of different age groups. The study found that youngsters are mostly like to eat modern food, spicy food and non-vegetarian food. The determinates of food choices such as good taste, healthy food ,low cost, good quality, and convenience food found very important for choose or like a food. This study also includes the food customs that must be followed while eating. Today the youngsters feels eating modern food such as pizza, burger prestige issue than buying the traditional food is oldest habit it also spoil food habits among youngster. This study is helpful for maintaining healthy diet and for building healthy food habits to keep our body healthy


Our survey revealed diverse food habits across age groups in Bethamcherla Mandal. Children and adolescents prefer fast foods like burgers and noodles, with a penchant for sweets. Young adults in their 20s and 30s explore both local and international cuisines, often opting for quick meals due to busy schedules, while increasingly prioritizing health with salads and lean proteins. Middle-aged adults focus on balanced, home-cooked meals, considering health factors and maintaining cultural food practices. Elderly individuals prefer lighter, traditional meals, emphasizing comfort and cultural culinary traditions. Overall, there's a growing interest in health and organic foods across all ages, influencing meal choices and eating patterns in the region.

we completed our Community Service Project (CSP) with immense joy and valuable learning experiences. Our project took us to several villages where we interacted closely with the local communities. The experience was both enlightening and enjoyable, filled with fun activities and meaningful engagements. During our visits, we focused on understanding and documenting the food habits of the villagers. We observed their daily routines, participated in meal preparations, and discussed their dietary practices. This provided us with deep insights into their nutritional choices, traditional recipes, and the cultural significance of their meals. The project not only allowed us to contribute positively to the communities but also enriched our own knowledge about rural life and sustainable living practices. It was a rewarding experience that combined education, service, and enjoyment, leaving us with cherished memories and a deeper appreciation for diverse lifestyles.

We conducted an awareness camp on food habits at schools, aiming to educate students about healthy eating practices. The camp included sessions on the importance of balanced nutrition, focusing on diverse food sources rich in essential nutrients like proteins, vitamins, and minerals. Through interactive workshops and informative presentations, we encouraged students to make informed choices about their diets, emphasizing the benefits of incorporating fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains into their daily meals. Our goal was to promote lifelong habits that support overall health and well-being among students, empowering them with the knowledge to make nutritious food choices both at school and beyond.
